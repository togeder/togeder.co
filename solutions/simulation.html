Use Case: Using Togeder Assess for Simulations and Targeted Learning Evaluation

Case Study May 2025

Overview

This use case illustrates how Togeder Assess can be used during live simulations to evaluate participants' knowledge application, critical thinking, and collaborative reasoning in real time. Togeder Assess is ideal for any learning environments where participants need to apply knowledge to solve complex problems. For example, in simulations in non-graded learning settings, like elective programs or workshopsâ€”where group dialogue, applied thinking, and instructor insight matter more than individual scores.

Goals

  • Evaluate how well participants apply previously learned knowledge collaborative problem-solving scenarios.
  • Identify gaps in understanding based on observed dialogue and decision-making processes.
  • Generate detailed report to inform targeted follow-up training or content review.
  • Encourage reflection and peer-to-peer learning through structured group activity.

Implementation Scenario

During a simulation (e.g., an emergency response role-play), participants are assigned to breakout rooms. Each group is given a case to resolve collaboratively. Togeder Assess is used to:

  • Monitor how participants analyze the situation, weigh options, and articulate their reasoning.
  • Measure participation and engagement levels within each group.
  • Evaluate the quality of reasoning and the relevance of the decisions made.
  • Capture dialogue summaries and key decision points through AI-powered transcription and tagging.

    • Assessment Criteria

    Togeder Assess can generate data for:

    • Knowledge Application: Did participants apply concepts accurately?
    • Reasoning Quality: Was their logic sound and evidence-based?
    • Group Dynamics: Did participants collaborate equitably?
    • Confidence Levels: Where was uncertainty or confusion visible?
    These metrics are reported immediately and can be mapped to learning objectives or certification rubrics.
